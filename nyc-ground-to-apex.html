The route, by altitude

Start with buried memory. Finish above the skyline.

This route is a climb, not a checklist: federal stone and ancestral ground, a civil-rights gallery, a show-room detonation, a glass-roof cocktail level, then the apex at Hudson Yards if City Climb returns to sale.

  1. 0ft

    Free

    African Burial Ground National Monument

    290 Broadway, New York, NY 10007

    Start where the city literally tried to bury its origin story. The memorial cuts through the federal-office grid and puts names, rites, and labor back into a place Wall Street would rather keep anonymous.

    MonTueWedThuFriSatSun Free entry

    Visitor center/museum 10 AM–4 PM on open days; outdoor memorial stays open until 5 PM in summer.

  2. 2F

    Heritage

    Jackie Robinson Museum

    75 Varick St, New York, NY 10013

    Jackie Robinson belongs in a museum because the story was never just second base. This stop pulls civil rights, media pressure, Brooklyn myth, and business discipline into one room—exactly the heritage check that keeps the climb honest.

    MonTueWedThuFriSatSun Adults $18

    Open 11 AM–6 PM Thu–Sun; timed tickets recommended, walk-ins possible.
